History of Evanescence

Evanescence started in 1995 in Arkansas with Amy Lee and Ben Moody. They became famous in the early 2000s for their mix of rock and gothic music. Their first big album was "Fallen" which came out in 2003, and it had hits like "Bring Me to Life" and "My Immortal." The band had some problems and some integrants left, but Amy Lee stayed as the main member. Evanescence is still remembered as a big part of 2000s music.

Discography

Fallen

The album was released on March 4, 2003. It was accepted and got positive reviews from critics. The most popular songs are: "Bring Me to Life", "Going Under", "My Inmortal", and "Everybody Fools"

The Open Door

The album was realesed on September 25, 2006. The band incorporate new sounds to their previous styles. The most popular songs are: "Sweet Sacrifice" and "Call Me When You're Sober".

Evanescence

The album was realesed on October 7, 2011. The band incorporate new sounds to their previous styles such as hard rock and symphonic metal. The most popular songs are: "What You Want" and "My Heart Is Broken".

Synthesis

The album was realesed on November 10, 2017. The band incorporate new sounds to their previous styles such as orchestral and electronica.

The Bitter Truth

The album was realesed on March 26, 2021. The band delayed the completion for pandemic.

Awards and Nominations

Evanescence is a famous rock band led by Amy Lee. They have won many awards and been nominated for even more throughout their career. They've won Grammys and MTV Music Awards, showing how much people love their music. One of their big wins was for their song "Bring Me to Life," which got them a Grammy in 2004. Their albums have sold a lot, and their songs are always on the charts, which is why they keep getting nominated for awards. It just goes to show that Evanescence is loved by fans and respected by the music industry.
